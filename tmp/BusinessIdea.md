## The Pitch

The BC consulting market is stuck in the past. Traditional service providers rely on large teams of consultants and developers, billing thousands of hours across bloated project timelines. Small and mid-sized businesses end up paying for more people than they actually need.

Your business changes that. By leveraging AI tools—Claude Code, automation agents, intelligent development workflows—one consultant delivers what traditionally takes a team of three to five. Faster implementations, lower total project costs for clients, higher margins for you.

The go-to-market: personal brand authority through YouTube, LinkedIn, and local in-person AI training. This creates both direct implementation clients and referral opportunities to partner firms in regions you can't serve directly.

---

## Phases at a Glance

**Phase 1 — Foundation (Months 1–6):** Build brand and content engine while full-time at Jeremias.

**Phase 2 — First Project (Months 6–15):** Part-time at Jeremias, deliver anchor project, prove the model.

**Phase 3 — First Scaling (Months 15–21):** First subcontractor, five new clients, establish quality control.

**Phase 4 — Growth (Months 21–30):** Three to four subcontractors, quality manager hire, ten to fifteen concurrent projects.

**Phase 5 — Full Scale (Year 3+):** Twenty concurrent projects, five to eight subcontractors, referral income, acquisition-ready.

---

## Phase 1 — Foundation (Months 1–6)

**Employment:** Full-time at Jeremias (45 hrs/week).

**What you do:** Launch YouTube channel focused on AI plus BC. Two videos per week, consistent LinkedIn posting. Offer free local AI crash courses within 30 km of Dinkelsbühl to build regional reputation. Identify potential subcontractors on Gulp and LinkedIn. Set up Microsoft Partner package, liability insurance, tax advisor. Scope and contract your friend's anchor project.

**Revenue:** Zero from consulting. Income is Jeremias salary at 130,000 euros plus company car. Business setup costs roughly 3,000 euros.

**Milestone:** YouTube channel live with consistent cadence, first local course delivered, anchor project contracted, two potential subcontractors identified.

---

## Phase 2 — First Project (Months 6–15)

**Employment:** Part-time Jeremias (22.5 hrs/week) + project (15 hrs/week) + content (7.5 hrs/week) = 45 hours total.

**What you do:** Deliver anchor BC implementation at your friend's company. Document everything—process, timeline, outcomes, cost savings versus traditional approach. This becomes your primary case study. Establish support partner relationship so your friend's company has ongoing coverage post-implementation. Continue YouTube and local courses.

**Revenue:** Jeremias part-time roughly 60,000 euros over twelve months. Anchor project roughly 50,000 to 80,000 euros. Total income 110,000 to 140,000 euros. Gap to your 160,000 target may require savings or higher billing rate.

**Milestone:** Anchor project successfully delivered, case study published, support partner in place, YouTube at 500 to 1,000 subscribers, first inbound leads arriving.

---

## Phase 3 — First Scaling (Months 15–21)

**Employment:** Full-time self-employed. Jeremias notice period initiated.

**What you do:** Leverage YouTube presence and case study to land five new implementation clients. Companies with 50 to 100 employees, 25 to 50 BC users. Average project: 600 to 1,000 hours over eight to twelve months. Onboard first subcontractor, train them on your methodology. Establish quality gates: weekly code reviews, milestone sign-offs, documentation standards. Begin referring leads you can't serve to partner firms at 5 to 10 percent referral fees.

**Revenue:** Total 465,000 to 780,000 euros. Subcontractor costs roughly 96,000 to 160,000. Your margin roughly 357,000 to 608,000 euros. Personal salary 160,000. Reserves 197,000 to 448,000 euros.

**Milestone:** Five active projects, one trained subcontractor, quality process documented, first referral income, YouTube at 2,000 to 4,000 subscribers.

---

## Phase 4 — Growth (Months 21–30)

**What you do:** Bring in two to three more subcontractors, staggered every two to three months. Hire a quality manager at roughly 75,000 euros annually to handle code reviews and oversight—this frees you from day-to-day technical management. Scale to ten to fifteen concurrent projects. Build recurring support contract revenue at 3,000 to 5,000 euros monthly per completed client. Scale referrals to five to eight annually.

**Revenue:** Total 2.3 to 3.0 million euros. Costs roughly 1.05 to 1.37 million. Gross margin 1.23 to 1.63 million. Your salary 160,000 to 250,000 euros. Remaining profit roughly 980,000 to 1.47 million euros.

**Milestone:** Quality manager in place, three to four subcontractors, ten to fifteen concurrent projects, recurring support revenue established, YouTube at 5,000 to 8,000 subscribers.

---

## Phase 5 — Full Scale (Year 3+)

**What you do:** Five to eight subcontractors, two to three quality managers, possibly one admin hire. You operate as CEO—strategic client relationships, content, business development. Minimal personal delivery. YouTube at 10,000 plus subscribers making you one of the most visible BC consultants in DACH. Potential Microsoft MVP recognition. Ten to fifteen referrals annually generating 120,000 to 225,000 euros in near-passive income.

**Revenue:** Total 3.4 to 5.2 million euros. Costs roughly 1.75 to 2.6 million. Gross margin 1.67 to 2.63 million. Your salary 250,000 to 400,000 euros. Company profit after salary 1.27 to 2.23 million euros.

**Exit options:** Acquisition valuation 1.5 to 3 million euros. Partnership or merger with larger BC firm also viable—you bring the brand and AI methodology, they bring scale and infrastructure.

**Milestone:** Recognized DACH brand, twenty concurrent projects, stable team, recurring revenue covering base costs, acquisition offers on the table.

---

## Key Risks and Mitigations

**Can't land clients after anchor project:** Low risk. Content strategy plus local training creates multiple lead channels.

**Subcontractor quality issues:** Medium risk. Mitigated by rigorous training, documentation, quality gates, and code reviews.

**Gaps between projects:** Medium risk. Support contracts provide recurring baseline revenue.

**Burnout:** Medium risk. Quality manager hire reduces your workload. Support partner covers client continuity.

**Content fatigue:** Medium risk. Batch creation and repurposing local training into YouTube content reduces effort.
